## Why Payment Processor Fees Matter

If you process $10,000 per month through a payment gateway, a 0.5% difference in fees costs you $600 per year. At $100,000 per month, that's $6,000. Choosing the right processor is not a minor decision.

## Stripe Fee Structure

Stripe charges a flat rate of 2.9% + $0.30 per successful card transaction for most markets. There are no monthly fees, no setup fees, and no minimum transaction requirements.

Additional costs apply for international cards (typically +1.5%), currency conversion (+1%), and certain payment methods like ACH or SEPA which have different flat rates and caps.

Stripe's pricing is transparent and predictable, which makes it a favorite among developers and startups.

## PayPal Fee Structure

PayPal's fees are more complex and vary by account type, transaction volume, and payment method. Standard transactions through PayPal Checkout typically run 3.49% + $0.49 for digital goods, or 2.99% + $0.49 for standard card transactions depending on your configuration.

PayPal does offer volume discounts for merchants processing over $3,000 per month, reducing rates progressively.

## Key Differences

**Developer experience:** Stripe has a significantly better API and developer documentation. Integration is faster and more flexible.

**Consumer trust:** PayPal has higher consumer recognition, especially for older demographics and international buyers who may not trust unfamiliar checkout forms.

**Dispute handling:** Both processors have merchant-unfriendly chargeback policies, but Stripe's resolution process is generally considered more transparent.

**Payout speed:** Stripe pays out in 2 business days by default. PayPal holds funds for varying periods especially for new accounts.

## Which Should You Choose?

For developer-built products and SaaS applications: Stripe. For marketplaces or businesses selling to older or international audiences who trust the PayPal brand: consider offering both. For the lowest possible fees at high volume: negotiate directly with a payment processor for custom rates.
